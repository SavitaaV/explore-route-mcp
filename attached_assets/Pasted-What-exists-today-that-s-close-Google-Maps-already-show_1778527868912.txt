What exists today that's close:
Google Maps already shows merchant listings along routes. Waze shows points of interest. Apple Maps has curated guides. Yelp and TripAdvisor surface nearby businesses. Shopify's own Shop app lets you discover merchants. Even the MCP concept — connecting AI agents to commerce data — is live through Shopify's existing partnerships with Claude and Perplexity.
So what's genuinely new here?
Let me be direct. The individual components exist. What doesn't exist is the specific combination and the specific problem it solves. But we need to be precise about what that problem actually is — because "local merchants are hard to find" is not precise enough to build something genuinely new.
The real unsolved problem underneath this idea:
Independent local merchants have two simultaneous crises that no existing product addresses together.
The first is the discovery crisis. A ceramics artist in Niagara-on-the-Lake has no scalable way to be found by the right person at the right moment. Google requires SEO sophistication and ad budget. Social media requires constant content creation. Word of mouth doesn't scale. The merchant is essentially invisible to everyone except people who already know they exist.
The second is the inventory truth crisis. Even when a merchant is discoverable, nobody knows what's actually in stock right now. Google shows opening hours. Yelp shows reviews. Nobody shows whether the specific lavender honey jar you want is actually on the shelf today. This is the ghost inventory problem — and it's what destroys trust in location-based commerce recommendations.
What's genuinely never been done:
The combination of three things simultaneously that no existing product does together.
One — intent-aware contextual discovery. Not "you're near a shop" but "you chose a scenic route therefore you are in discovery mode therefore the right moment to surface a merchant is now, not when you searched for it." The route choice is the intent signal. No product uses route context as a commerce intent signal feeding into an AI agent layer.
Two — real-time inventory confidence with crowdsourced verification that decays. Not static "this shop sells ceramics" but "right now, three people who visited in the last four hours confirmed the spring collection is in stock, confidence score 87%." This specific mechanism — time-decayed crowdsourced inventory verification feeding a confidence score that governs whether a recommendation fires — does not exist anywhere.
Three — merchant digital twin creation from discovery. When the crowd finds a physical merchant not yet on Shopify, the system creates a draft profile and invites the merchant to claim it. This turns every explorer into an unpaid merchant acquisition channel for Shopify. No existing product converts physical discovery into merchant onboarding automatically.
The genuinely new value proposition:
Explore Route MCP is not a navigation product with commerce features. It's not a merchant discovery app. It's a merchant acquisition and activation channel disguised as a consumer experience.
From Shopify's perspective the real value is not the consumer journey. It's that every explorer becomes a signal generator that finds undigitised merchants, verifies inventory, and pulls new businesses onto the Shopify platform organically. The consumer experience is the front door. The merchant acquisition flywheel is the actual business.
Where it could go that's truly unprecedented:
Right now Shopify's agentic commerce layer — MCP, Universal Cart, Checkout Kit — is entirely triggered by text search intent. Someone types "I want a gift" into Claude or Perplexity and Shopify merchants surface. That's the only trigger that exists.
Explore Route MCP would add a second trigger type that has never existed in Shopify's infrastructure — spatial intent. Movement through physical space as a commerce trigger. This is a new primitive. If it works, it doesn't just help Niagara-on-the-Lake ceramics studios get found. It creates a new category of commerce interaction that Shopify could patent, license to navigation platforms, and build an entirely new revenue line around.
The Google Maps integration angle is particularly important here. Google makes $9.1 billion annually from Maps advertising. Almost none of that reaches independent SMB merchants because the auction dynamics price them out. Explore Route MCP sitting as a layer between Google Maps routing and Shopify's catalog creates a commerce channel for merchants who are structurally excluded from the existing Maps monetisation model. That is genuinely new and genuinely valuable.
The honest question you need to answer before building:
Is the core insight — that route choice is a high-quality commerce intent signal that no existing product captures — actually true in a way that's defensible and buildable?
I believe it is. But here is the test. Ask yourself: when you have chosen a scenic drive rather than a highway, were you more open to stopping somewhere unexpected than on a regular commute? If the answer is yes — and I suspect it is — then the intent signal is real and the product premise holds.
What would make this genuinely world-class:
Add one more layer that nobody has discussed yet. Merchant storytelling through the agent.
Right now every merchant discovery product shows you a rating and a category. Four stars. Ceramics. That tells you nothing that makes you feel something.
What if the Claude agent, when surfacing a merchant, told you one true thing about them that made you want to stop? Not a review summary. A story. "She's been throwing pots on this street for 22 years. She learned from her grandmother in Portugal. The spring collection she just finished took four months." That story comes from the merchant's own Shopify store description, their social media, their past customer reviews — synthesised by the agent into one human sentence that creates genuine desire to stop.
No navigation product, no merchant discovery app, no commerce platform does this today. And it's the difference between a feature and an experience someone remembers and tells their friends about.
My honest recommendation before building the prototype:
Sharpen the one-liner first. Right now if someone asks "what is Explore Route MCP" the answer takes three paragraphs. For Shopify APM application and for the prototype to land, you need one sentence that is undeniably clear.
Here is a candidate:
"Explore Route MCP is a Shopify spatial commerce layer that converts a traveller's route choice into a real-time merchant discovery signal — surfacing independent local businesses through AI agents at the exact moment a person is most open to finding them, with crowdsourced inventory verification that ensures every recommendation is worth stopping for."
