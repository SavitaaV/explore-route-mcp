You’ve nailed the key distinction. The Shopify Catalog MCP is a passive library; your product is an Active Intelligence Layer.
You are building the Autonomous Commerce Router (ACR). It doesn’t sit inside Shopify (as a store tool) or inside Google (as a map feature). It sits as Middleware in the Cloud that acts as the "Traffic Controller" between them.
## 1. Where it sits: The "Commerce Edge"
Your product should be hosted as an Independent MCP Service on a high-availability cloud (like Vercel or AWS).

* The Connection: It maintains persistent "Websocket" or "SSE" connections to both the Google Maps Routes API (for the user's path) and the Shopify Global Catalog (for the inventory).
* The Hub: It serves as the Graph Database that stores the "Shadow Edges" (the links between the Google pins and the Shopify stores).

## 2. Your Product Offering: "Anticipatory Discovery"
If an interviewer asks, "What do you offer?", your answer is: "I offer Zero-Prompt Acquisition."
Your product isn't a search tool; it's a Matchmaking Engine with three core components:

* The "Shadow Node" Onboarder: It scans the user's Google Maps route and identifies "Siloed Nodes" (non-Shopify merchants) that should be part of the graph. It generates a pre-filled Shopify Draft Store the moment a high-intent user passes by.
* The Proximity Trigger: Instead of waiting for a user to ask "Where's coffee?", your ACR detects the "Vibe Match" (e.g., user is 4 hours into a drive + likes local roasters) and pushes the recommendation to their device's Agent.
* The Unified Settlement Layer: It allows a user to buy from any merchant in the graph—even the "Shadow Nodes"—by using Shopify Bill Pay as a proxy. This is how you force the non-Shopify merchant to join: "You have a sale waiting. Claim it on Shopify."

## 3. How this specifically helps Shopify (The "Senior PM" pitch)

* For Acquisition: It turns Google Maps into a Lead-Gen machine. You aren't pitching merchants to "build a website"; you are showing them unclaimed money sitting on a map.
* For Transactions: It captures the "Impulse Detour" market. By showing the right item at the right time on the right route, you create sales that wouldn't have happened on a standard e-commerce site.
* For Retention: It makes Shopify merchants part of a "Neighborhood Network." They stay because they are receiving autonomous referrals from the system without lifting a finger.
